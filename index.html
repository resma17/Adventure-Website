Sands and Summits!

Summertime at Beach

A beach is a narrow strip of land separating a body of water from inland areas. Beaches are usually made of sand, tiny grains of rocks and minerals that have been worn down by constant pounding by wind and waves. This beach, in Pebble Beach, California, has both sandy and rocky features.

Mountain

The mighty chunks rise all over the world, including the oceans. They usually have steep, sloping sides and sharp ridges, and a high point, called a summit. Most geologists classify a mountain as a landform that rises at least 1,000 feet or more above its surrounding area.
